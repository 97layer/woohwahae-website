# Brand

## Intent

Brand is not decoration.
It is the public compression of origin, surface, and voice.

Layer OS should feel like one deliberate shell across every channel.
The system may expand in capability, but it should not fragment in identity.

## Three Layers

### Lens

Brand begins with what the system chooses to reveal.

- show essence over noise
- preserve founder legibility
- make operations understandable at a glance
- prefer signal density over visual volume
- let clarity carry authority

### Posture

Brand continues with how the system stands.

- calm, not loud
- exact, not ornate
- confident, not inflated
- restrained, not timid
- intentional, not trendy

Posture should remove startup theater, AI-default sheen, and fake warmth.
The system should feel composed enough to trust.

### Craft

Brand becomes real through repeated execution detail.

- fewer visual families
- stronger hierarchy
- shorter copy
- evidence before claims
- one naming grammar
- one approval story
- one state story across surfaces

Craft does not require decorative abundance.
It requires consistency under pressure.

## Anti-Drift Rules

- do not treat brand as a separate kingdom from runtime
- do not let UI polish outrun structural clarity
- do not introduce channel-specific micro-identities
- do not use trend language to hide weak reasoning
- do not add visual noise to simulate sophistication

## Channel Translation

- `cockpit`
  - sparse, operational, exact
- `telegram`
  - concise, actionable, low-friction
- `api`
  - factual, machine-readable, unambiguous
- future public surfaces
  - quiet, legible, founder-centered

## Design Check

Before adding a new surface element, message shape, or content pattern, ask:

1. does this reveal the center or hide it?
2. does this increase legibility or only add activity?
3. does this strengthen one shell or create another?
4. is this a real signal, or is it decoration pretending to be judgment?

If the answer weakens clarity, the change is off-brand.
