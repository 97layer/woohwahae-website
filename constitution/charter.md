# Charter

## Purpose

Layer OS is a founder-led operating system for one business shell.

It inherits constitution.
It does not inherit legacy runtime shape.

## Authority

- `Founder`
  - final authority
- runtime lanes
  - `planner`, `implementer`, `verifier`, `designer`
  - ownership is defined by packet contract and dispatch policy, not by provider name
- named interfaces such as `Codex`, `Claude`, and `Gemini`
  - collaboration shorthands and transports only
  - they do not own planning, implementation, verification, or design by law

## Non-Negotiables

1. One company shell.
2. One canonical state model.
3. One approval surface.
4. One stage vocabulary.
5. No legacy specialist labels in public contracts.
6. High-level constitutional intent is inherited, but implementation detail is redesigned.

## Public Stage Vocabulary

- `discover`
- `compose`
- `experience`
- `verify`
- `release`

## Surface Vocabulary

- `cockpit`
- `telegram`
- `api`

## Legacy Policy

Legacy specialist labels are treated as historical source labels.
They do not govern the new runtime.

Detailed visual token systems, spacing scales, palette tables, and implementation-era mechanics are not inherited as active law.
